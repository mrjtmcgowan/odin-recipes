The Best Tacos Ever

About the Dish

The tacos you can create with this recipe will beat any you've ever tried. The first step is using tortillas from a local shop.

Open your phone (if you aren't already using it as you read this) and navigate yourself to a "tortilleria".

If you've never been to a Latino/Mexican/Salvadoran/etc. market don't be intimmidated when you walk in. Other than having excellent produce you'll find, usually at the back of the store, a tortilleria: that magical machine that creates true tortillas.

*Side note: the "crunchy" tacos that you might find in sad yellow boxes on the shelves of Walmart or other similar stores are unacceptable for human consumption.

Acquire the tortillas and get double the amount of tacos you're making. Acquire the other ingredients wherever you like, but you're alredy in a store so why not get some of that great produce, eh?

  • you preferred meat (chicken/beef/chorizo/etc.) or you can sub mushrooms (organic is a must here)

Once you've purchased all of your ingredients complete the following steps.

  1. In a large skillet, combine the chicken, salsa, beans, onion powder, chili powder and remaining cumin. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until chicken juices run clear.
  2. In an ungreased skillet, cook tortillas, one at a time, until lightly browned, about 30 seconds on each side.
  3. Layer between pieces of waxed paper or paper towel; keep warm. Spoon chicken mixture over half of each tortilla and fold over. Serve with lettuce, cheese, olives, sour cream and salsa if desired.
